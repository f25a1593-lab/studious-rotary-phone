The Bioscience Scenario

Harumanis Mango (*Mangifera indica* L.) Fruit Development

In Perlis, Malaysia, the premium Harumanis mango growth cycle takes approximately 110 to 120 days after anthesis (DAA). Modeling the growth curve of the mango allows agrotechnologists to calculate the exact instantaneous growth rate of the fruit.

By calculating the derivative of the weight function, $\frac{dW}{dt}$ or $f'(t)$, we can identify the transition points between the lag phase (slow cell division), the rapid cell expansion phase (highest growth rate), and the ripening phase. This mathematical insight dictates precision irrigation and fertilizer application schedules.

Mathematical Framework

The Logistic Growth Function

Biological growth is modeled elegantly using a sigmoidal Logistic curve, where growth slows down as resources or biological limits are reached:

$$f(t) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$

Where:
• $f(t)$ = Fruit Weight ($\text{grams}$) at day $t$
• $L = 450\text{g}$ (Asymptote - Typical Premium Harumanis Mass)
• $k = 0.08$ (Growth rate coefficient)
• $t_0 = 65\text{ DAA}$ (Inflection point - Midpoint of maximum expansion rate)

The First Derivative $f'(t)$

Using the quotient rule, the derivative represents the instantaneous growth rate ($\text{grams/day}$) at any specific day $t$:

$$f'(t) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t-t_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(t-t_0)})^2}$$

Alternatively represented via state feedback in bioscience: $$f'(t) = k \cdot f(t) \cdot \left(1 - \frac{f(t)}{L}\right)$$ This indicates that growth speed is directly proportional to current size and the remaining growth capacity.
